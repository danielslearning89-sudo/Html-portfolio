The Gretest Animes According to Daniel

Top Three Greatest animes

Fullmetal Alchemist: Brotherhood

A near-perfect blend of action, philosophy, and emotional storytelling. It follows brothers Edward and Alphonse Elric, who use alchemy in a quest to restore their bodies after a failed ritual. Along the way, they uncover a deep political conspiracy involving their country's military and a mysterious group of beings.
Why it stands out: Tight plot, powerful themes (sacrifice, morality, human life), and a satisfying ending.

Attack on Titan

Set in a world where humanity hides behind giant walls to survive monstrous Titans, this story starts as a survival thriller and evolves into a complex narrative about freedom, war, and human nature. It follows Eren Yeager and his friends as they fight for survival—and uncover shocking truths about their world.
Why it stands out: Intense action, unpredictable twists, and increasingly deep political and moral questions.

Death Note

A psychological battle of wits between a genius high school student, Light Yagami, and a mysterious detective known as L. After Light gains a notebook that allows him to kill anyone by writing their name, he attempts to reshape the world into his version of justice.
Why it stands out: Gripping mind games, moral ambiguity, and one of anime's most iconic rivalries.
